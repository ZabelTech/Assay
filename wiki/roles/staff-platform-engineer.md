# Staff platform engineer

A senior individual-contributor track focused on multi-team platform work:
shared infrastructure, internal developer surfaces, and cross-cutting
reliability concerns. Distinct from a manager-equivalent staff role; this
page describes the IC archetype.

## Signal

> sources: 1, 2, 3

- Multi-team scope: changes ship through several team boundaries, not just
  the author's own.
- Architectural leadership: drives platform RFCs, design reviews, or
  technical strategy across teams.
- On-call ownership of a shared surface (CI, deploy pipeline, primary data
  store, identity infra).
- Hands-on coding remains a sizeable share of the week (typically a third
  or more); the role is not a manager-track stepping stone.
- Often the technical lead for incidents that span teams; named in the
  postmortem as the recovery driver.

## Corroborating evidence

> sources: 2, 3, 4

- `employment` claims with staff/principal-level titles and ≥5 years at the
  same employer (deep context tends to matter for the role).
- `project` claims describing platforms (CI/CD, observability, identity,
  scheduling) rather than feature work.
- `endorsement` claims from cross-functional partners: product managers,
  engineering managers in adjacent teams, SREs.
- `publication` claims (internal RFCs surfaced as blog posts, conference
  talks at SRECon / QCon / etc.).
- `credential` claims are weak signal here; the title is in-band evidence,
  not certification.

## Caveats

> sources: 1, 2

- Title scope varies dramatically between startups and large companies.
  At a 50-person startup, "staff" sometimes maps to what a FAANG would call
  senior; at FAANG, "staff" is its own well-defined rung.
- "Staff" sometimes means individual-contributor terminal level, sometimes
  means manager-equivalent. The IC archetype is what this page targets; an
  endorser describing "Staff Engineer (people manager)" is a different
  role.
- Platform vs. product is a meaningful axis even within IC staff. Product
  staff engineers ship customer-facing features at scale; platform staff
  engineers build the surfaces other teams use. Both are legitimate; they
  cite different evidence.
